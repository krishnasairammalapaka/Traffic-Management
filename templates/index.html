Important Traffic Rules in India

Be a responsible driver by knowing and following these crucial Indian traffic laws:

  • Rule 15 (Speed Limits): According to Section 112 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, drivers must adhere to speed limits set for different areas and vehicle types. Exceeding speed limits can lead to fines or license suspension.
  • Rule 125 (Wearing Seat Belts): Rule 125 mandates seatbelt usage for all vehicle occupants. Non-compliance can lead to fines and poses serious safety risks.
  • Rule 129 (Helmet Mandate for Two-Wheelers): Section 129 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 requires all riders of two-wheelers to wear helmets. Violations can incur fines and penalties.
  • Rule 177 (Violation of Traffic Signals): Failure to follow traffic signals is punishable under Section 177 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, with penalties including fines and, for repeated offenses, license suspension.
  • Rule 194D (Use of Mobile Devices While Driving): Using mobile phones while driving is strictly prohibited. This rule aims to prevent distractions that lead to accidents.
